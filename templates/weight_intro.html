Don't let gym bros intimidate you with their fancy talk, here's a quick introduction to using weights!
  • Rep: a single execution of an exercise (short for repetition)
  • Set: a collection of reps that you do consecutively before resting
  • 1-Rep Max: the maximum amount of weight you can possibly lift for only 1 rep; this means that the weight is strenuous enough that you can't do more than a single rep in a set
  • General Rules:
    • you should start with 50%-60% of your 1-rep max to avoid injury
    • it is always better to start with less weight and then build up gradually set-by-set
    • for most exercise machines, you increase difficulty by increasing weight; however, for the adjusted pull-up machine, you increase difficulty by decreasing weight because this means it is helping you less
    • you don't need to complete more than 3-4 sets for each exercise
    • if you want to build muscle, focus on more weight but fewer reps (roughly 6-8 reps per set)
    • if you want to tone muscle, focus on less weight but more reps (roughly 13-15 reps per set)
